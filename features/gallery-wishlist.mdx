The gallery gives you a searchable list of every weapon skin in the game. From here you can build a wishlist of skins you want, and optionally receive a push notification the moment one of them appears in your daily shop.

## Browsing the gallery

The gallery loads all skins from the local Valorant asset data. Only skins that have a content tier (i.e. paid skins with a rarity) are shown — default and level-0 skins are excluded.

### Searching

Type into the search bar at the top to filter skins by name. The search is case-insensitive and uses a 100 ms debounce, so the list updates shortly after you stop typing.

### Sort order

Skins are sorted so that your wishlisted skins always appear first. Within each group (wishlisted and non-wishlisted), skins retain their default order from the asset data.

## Managing the wishlist

Each skin row has a **⋮** (three-dot) menu on the right side. Tap it to open a context menu with the following options:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Add to wishlist / Remove from wishlist">
    Toggles the skin's wishlist status. Wishlisted skins show a ⭐ prefix in the gallery and in the daily shop when they appear as an offer.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Levels">
    Opens a media popup showing each upgrade level's display icon or streamed video.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Chromas">
    Opens a media popup showing each chroma variant's full render or streamed video.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Persistence

Your wishlist is saved to **AsyncStorage** using Zustand's persist middleware under the key `wishlist`. It survives app restarts and does not require a network connection.

## Background notifications

VShop can check your daily shop in the background and notify you when a wishlisted skin is available.

<Warning>
  Background notifications are a **donator-only** feature. You must be a verified donator for the notification check to run.
</Warning>

### How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable notifications in Settings">
    Turn on the wishlist notification toggle in the app's Settings screen. This sets `notificationEnabled` to `true` in your local wishlist store.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Background fetch runs automatically">
    The app registers a background fetch task that runs at a minimum interval of 15 minutes (subject to OS scheduling). Each time the task fires it checks whether a full shop check is needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Once-per-day check (UTC)">
    The app compares today's UTC date against the timestamp stored in AsyncStorage under the key `lastWishlistCheck`. If the dates differ (or no check has ever run), it fetches the current daily shop.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Notification is sent">
    If a wishlisted skin is found in the shop, you receive a notification naming the skin. If no match is found, you receive a notification confirming none of your wishlisted skins are in today's shop. If an error occurs, an error notification is sent instead.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Notification details

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Android notification channel | `wishlist` |
| Channel importance | Maximum |
| Sound | Yes |
| Badge | No |

<Note>
  The background check runs at most once per calendar day (UTC). Multiple background fetch invocations on the same day will skip the shop check after the first successful run.
</Note>

<Info>
  The background task uses the stored session cookie to silently re-authenticate with Riot's servers. No manual action is required after initial login.
</Info>
